Sailing via sick certificate

It is said that sailing is balm for the soul. Escape from everyday life. In this context, "cast off" is also a household word, even among landlubbers. It stands for starting anew, setting yourself free, thinking differently - even if only for a short time -, points of view rethink, rearrange, the familiar, the everyday environment moves eighth, gets smaller until nothing can be seen behind the horizon, regardless of whether the trip lasts a few hours, an afternoon or many days and weeks. No matter whether on inland or high seas, dinghy or big ship, whether sporty or leisurely. The effect may be different, but in principle the same. Only recently was even the ordinary judiciary concerned with the question of whether it is with This salutary effect of sailing has something to do with it. Essentially, it was about whether a medically certified burn-out disease can be successfully treated while sailing. An employee was ill for two and a half months and was given sick leave and then went to Croatia to sail. The termination without notice followed. The judges found it wrong after the attending physician testified that she had expressly advised her patient. The Düsseldorf State Labor Court followed the argument of the doctor - in the deep inland. So it is on record: sailing is balm for the soul. But are things really that simple? Then, when in doubt, the stressed city dweller only needed to sit on a boat and get some fresh air. In the end, the health insurance company would have to pay for antifouling or take on charter costs. And why hasn't the entire stress-ridden nation been sailing long ago - actually irresponsible not to do so. Perhaps there are limits to this fountain of youth after all. How far has it come with the healing effects of sailing, who does it affect, and what needs to be done to ensure that the time on board remains a lasting experience?

Admittedly, the 73-year-old Swede Sven Yrvind is an extreme example. He is preparing himself for these days the non-stop circumnavigation of the world on a boat ten feet long. That's 3.10 meters. Needless to say, he'll sit alone in that bathtub. Yrvind is not an aging weirdo. He has crossed oceans several times Taken behind him without accidents in self-designed and built sailing canoes, he has held the record for circumnavigating Cape Horn in the smallest boat since 1980.

New Shores

What everyone unites is a certain regularity in what they do. And according to Dr. Ewald Piel is also important when it comes to creating an effective balance from everyday life. Piel is not alone in his opinion. The educator Kerstin Möller has also discovered sailing as a suitable medium for her health education courses. On the flat-bottomed ship “Anna Lisa” she offers a “Sailing weekend for health maintenance” under the title “Resource Travel”. Möller works primarily with the environment. From Glückstadt it goes off the Elbe, including dry traps. "The mudflats as living space offer the ideal environment for my purposes," she says. "People are constrained by the constraints of their professional life. I confront them with the vastness of the Wadden Sea and observe how they deal with it." She has worked in art therapy for a long time. It is important to her to depict problems. In the mud flats it is the case that the course participants mark out rooms, the size corresponds to what they perceive to be free space. Then they should mark out how big The maritime environment on board also offers a lot of metaphors. “A passing container ship only comes towards you, looks threatening, but disappears astern, becomes smaller and smaller and soon won't be seen anymore. ”Möller speaks in a calm voice, but when she talks about this cornucopia of pictures, she sounds enthusiastic. Knots would be untied, new shores discovered, a breath of fresh air let into one's own world of thought.
